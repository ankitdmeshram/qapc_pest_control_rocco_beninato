How to Prepare for Bed Bug Control Treatment

To allow our bed bug exterminators to accomplish their job effectively, preparation is essential. Please fill out the checklist below so that we can provide the best bed bug treatment possible.

Bed Bug Control Preparation Check List:

  • Remove clutter to minimise the number of hiding spots for bedbugs. Any goods you no longer use should be bagged and discarded in outside rubbish cans.
  • Remove all bedding, linens, comforters, and pillows from your beds and place them in plastic bags to be washed.
  • Mattresses and box springs should be free of any plastic covers. Bed insect infestations are not protected by plastic covers on mattresses or box springs.
  • Remove all clothing and other items from drawers, nightstands, closets, and other storage locations, and arrange them in plastic bags to be washed.
  • Take down the drapes in the bedrooms. Also, place any loose items or garments found outside of dressers, under mattresses, or in closets in plastic bags to be washed. Remove all paintings, photos, posters, and mirrors off the walls of your bedroom.
  • Remove vacuum bags and place them in outside trash cans. For extermination treatment, your vacuum and other electrical appliances should stay in place.
  • Any items stored under the bed should be thrown away or left in place for the bed bug removal treatment.
  • Aquariums can be left in place for at least six hours if the filtration and aeration systems are switched off and the tank is adequately covered.
  • After the bed bug exterminator leaves, all tenants, including dogs, must leave the unit and not return for at least 4 to 6 hours. Instructions for Laundry:
  • Empty all laundry bags into the washing machine immediately and wash everything in a hot water cycle.
  • Use outdoor garbage cans to dispose of discarded plastic bags.
  • Some goods that can't be washed can be disinfected successfully in the dryer. Duffel bags, pocketbooks, backpacks, teddy animals, and other soft objects are examples.
